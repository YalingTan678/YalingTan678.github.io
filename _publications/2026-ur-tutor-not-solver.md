## Study Overview

This paper presents the design and evaluation of a guardrailed AI assistant built for learning contexts in higher education. Unlike general-purpose AI tools that readily provide answers, this system is designed to function as a tutor — guiding students through problem-solving processes rather than delivering solutions.

## Design Principles

- **Scaffolding over answering**: The AI provides hints, asks guiding questions, and breaks down complex problems
- **Guardrails**: Built-in constraints prevent the AI from completing assignments or providing direct answers
- **Adaptive support**: The system adjusts its level of scaffolding based on student progress and needs

## Key Contributions

- A practical framework for designing educationally-aligned AI assistants
- Evidence on student learning outcomes with guardrailed vs. unrestricted AI tools
- Design guidelines for institutions seeking to integrate AI responsibly
