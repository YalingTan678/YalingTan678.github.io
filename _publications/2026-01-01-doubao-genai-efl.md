## Abstract

In response to China's AI literacy policy for primary and secondary education, this study examines senior high school students' experiences and perceptions of using Doubao, a domestic generative AI tool, for English writing feedback. This mixed-methods case study involved **90 Grade 11 EFL students** from a provincial model high school in China over one semester. Data were collected through pre/post-test writing assessments, a 20-item Course Experience Questionnaire (CEQ), and interviews.

## Key Findings

- **High CEQ reliability** (α = 0.950) and **significant improvement** in writing scores from pre-test (M = 21.72) to post-test (M = 24.50), with medium effect size (d<sub>z</sub> = 0.60)
- **Doubao's practical value**: instant feedback, content support, and vocabulary/grammar assistance
- **Limitations identified**: contextual misinterpretations and insufficient sensitivity to individual learning trajectories
- **Experiential differences**: students clearly distinguished between AI feedback (lower-order: grammar, vocabulary) and human teacher feedback (higher-order: rhetoric, argumentation)

## Theoretical Framework

Grounded in **Vygotsky's ZPD and scaffolding theory**, the study frames AI and teachers as complementary mediators. It synthesizes the **4T Pyramid Model** (Jin et al., 2025) for taxonomizing AI interactions, and the **Cognitive Model of Multimodal Writing** (Liu et al., 2024), scaffolding students to assume the *Evaluator* role for critical reflection on AI outputs.

## The Integrated AI-Human Collaborative Model

The study proposes a **four-stage pedagogical model** for structured AI-teacher sequencing:
1. **Pre-writing**: Teacher guidance on effective AI prompting strategies
2. **Drafting**: Students use Doubao for initial feedback on grammar and vocabulary
3. **Revision**: Teacher provides higher-order feedback on rhetoric, argumentation, and organization
4. **Reflection**: Students critically evaluate AI suggestions and develop metacognitive skills

## Method

- **Design**: Mixed-methods case study with pre-post design
- **Participants**: 90 EFL students (44 male, 46 female), aged 16–17
- **Duration**: One academic semester (Feb–Jun 2025)
- **Instruments**: Pre/post writing tests, 20-item CEQ (Likert scale), student reflections, interviews

## Implications

The study concludes that Doubao demonstrates potential as a **supplementary tool** in writing instruction when paired with structured teacher guidance. However, its impact may differ across student proficiency levels and instructional contexts. The findings contribute empirical evidence to AI-assisted writing education at the secondary level — an underexplored area in AIED research.

**Keywords**: Generative AI, Doubao, EFL writing, AI-assisted feedback, AI-human collaboration
