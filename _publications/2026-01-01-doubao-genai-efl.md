## Abstract

This study examines senior high school students' experiences using **Doubao**, a domestic generative AI tool, for English writing feedback. A mixed-methods case study involving **90 Grade 11 EFL students** over one semester.

**At a glance:** 90 students · CEQ reliability α = 0.950 · Score improvement +2.78 · Effect size d = 0.60

## The Integrated AI-Human Collaborative Model

![AI-Human Collaborative Model](/images/pubs/doubao-model.jpg)

*The four-stage pedagogical model for structured AI-teacher sequencing:*

| Stage | Focus | Role |
|:------|:------|:-----|
| **1. Pre-writing** | Teacher guides AI prompting strategies | Teacher-led |
| **2. Drafting** | Doubao provides grammar & vocabulary feedback | AI-assisted |
| **3. Revision** | Teacher gives higher-order feedback on rhetoric | Teacher-led |
| **4. Reflection** | Students critically evaluate AI suggestions | Student-led |

## Writing Score Improvement

| | Pre-test | Post-test | Change |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Mean Score** | 21.72 | 24.50 | **+2.78** |
| **Effect Size** | | | d = 0.60 (medium) |

## Key Findings

- **Doubao's value**: Instant feedback, content support, vocabulary/grammar assistance
- **Limitations**: Contextual misinterpretations, insufficient sensitivity to individual trajectories
- **AI vs. Human**: Students clearly distinguished between AI (lower-order) and teacher (higher-order) feedback
- **Conclusion**: Doubao works best as a **supplementary tool** with structured teacher guidance

**Keywords**: Generative AI, Doubao, EFL writing, AI-assisted feedback, AI-human collaboration
