## Presentation Summary

Presented findings from a comparative study of human and machine interpreting at the XXIInd International Computer Assisted Language Learning (CALL) Conference in Tokyo, Japan.

## Key Points

- Applied Relevance Theory to analyze context-awareness in simultaneous interpreting
- Compared outputs from human interpreters with Google Speech Translation and iFlytek
- Demonstrated significant differences in contextual adaptation between human and machine interpreting
- Discussed implications for AI-assisted language learning and interpreting education
